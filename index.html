Professional Sports

A history of the Big 5 sports leagues in North America

Here's a timeline of professional sports leagues in the United States. Let us begin:

  • 1876 - National League is established
  • 1900 - National League "Classic Eight" lineup of teams is established: Chicago Cubs, Boston Braves, Brooklyn Dodgers, New York Giants, Philadelphia Phillies, Pittsburgh Pirates, Cincinnati Reds, and St. Louis Cardinals
  • 1901 - American League is established with eight teams: Boston Red Sox, Chicago White Sox, Cleveland Indians, Detroit Tigers, Philadelphia Athletics, Washington Senators, Milwaukee Brewers, and Baltimore Orioles
  • 1902 - Milwaukee Brewers move to St. Louis and become the Browns
  • 1903 - Baltimore Orioles move to New York and become the Yankees
  • 1917 - National Hockey League is established
  • 1920 - National Football League is established (only two original teams remain: Chicago Cardinals, Decatur Staleys)
  • 1921 - Decatur Staleys move to Chicago and become the Bears the next year; Green Bay Packers join NFL
  • 1925 - New York Giants (NFL) created as expansion team
  • 1930 - Portsmouth Spartans (NFL) created as expansion team
  • 1932 - Boston Redskins (NFL) created as expansion team
  • 1933 - Philadelphia Eagles and Pittsburgh Steelers (both NFL) created as expansion teams
  • 1934 - Portsmouth Spartans move to Detroit and become the Lions
  • 1937 - Boston Redskins move to Washington; Cleveland Rams join NFL from AFL II
  • 1942 - National Hockey League "Original Six" lineup of teams is established: Montreal Canadiens, Toronto Maple Leafs, Boston Bruins, Chicago Blackhawks, Detroit Red Wings, and New York Rangers
  • 1946 - Cleveland Rams move to Los Angeles
  • 1949 - National Basketball Association is established which includes eight current teams: New York Knicks, Boston Celtics, Philadelphia Warriors, Minneapolis Lakers, Rochester Royals, Fort Wayne Pistons, Tri-Cities Blackhawks, and Syracuse Nationals
  • 1950 - Cleveland Browns and San Francisco 49ers join NFL from AAFC
  • 1951 - Tri-Cities Blackhawks move to Milwaukee and become the Hawks
  • 1953 - Boston Braves move to Milwaukee and become the Brewers; Baltimore Colts (NFL) created as expansion team
  • 1954 - St. Louis Browns move to Baltimore and become the Orioles
  • 1955 - Philadelphia Athletics move to Kansas City; Milwaukee Hawks move to St. Louis
  • 1957 - Rochester Royals move to Cincinnati; Fort Wayne Pistons move to Detroit
  • 1958 - New York Giants move to San Francisco; Brooklyn Dodgers move to Los Angeles
  • 1960 - Chicago Cardinals move to St. Louis; Dallas Cowboys (NFL) created as expansion teams; Minneapolis Lakers move to Los Angeles; American Football League is established with eight teams: Dallas Texans, Los Angeles Chargers, Denver Broncos, New York Jets, Boston Patriots, Oakland Raiders, Houston Oilers, and Buffalo Bills
  • 1961 - Los Angeles Chargers move to San Diego; Chicago Packers/Zephyrs (NBA) and Minnesota Vikings (NFL) created as expansion teams; Continental League established with eight teams: New York Mets, Houston Astros, Denver Bears, Minnesota Twins, Atlanta Shamrocks, Buffalo Bisons, Dallas Rangers, and Toronto Maple Leafs
  • 1962 - Philadelphia Warriors move to San Francisco
  • 1963 - Syracuse Nationals move to Philadelphia and become the 76ers; Chicago Packers/Zephyrs move to Baltimore and become the Bullets; Dallas Texans move to Kansas City and become the Chiefs
  • 1965 - Denver Bears move to Anaheim and become the California Angels
  • 1966 - Chicago Bulls (NBA), Atlanta Falcons (NFL), and Miami Dolphins (AFL) created as expansion teams
  • 1967 - Seattle Supersonics (NBA), San Diego Rockets (NBA), and New Orleans Saints (NFL) created as expansion teams; American Basketball Association is established; National Hockey League "Expansion Six" is established with six expansion teams: California Golden Seals, Los Angeles Kings, Minnesota North Stars, Philadelphia Flyers, Pittsburgh Penguins, and St. Louis Blues
  • 1968 - St. Louis Hawks move to Atlanta; Cincinnati Bengals (AFL), Milwaukee Bucks (NBA), and Phoenix Suns (NBA) created as expansion teams
  • 1969 - Kansas City Athletics move to Oakland; CL-AL-NL merger; Major League Baseball is established with the American and National Leagues; eight teams absorbed from the CL: New York Mets (NL), Houston Astros (NL), (Anaheim) California Angels (AL), Minnesota Twins (AL), Atlanta Braves (NL), Buffalo Bisons (NL), (Dallas) Texas Rangers (AL), and Toronto Blue Jays (AL)
  • 1970 - Buffalo Braves, (NBA), Buffalo Sabres (NHL), Cleveland Cavaliers (NBA), Portland Trail Blazers (NBA), and Vancouver Canucks (NHL) created as expansion teams; AFL-NFL merger; American Football Conference and National Football Conference established with thirteen teams each: (AFC: Baltimore Colts, Boston Patriots, Buffalo Bills, Miami Dolphins, New York Jets, Cincinnati Bengals, Cleveland Browns, Houston Oilers, Pittsburgh Steelers, Denver Broncos, Kansas City Chiefs, Oakland Raiders, and San Diego Chargers), (NFC: Dallas Cowboys, New York Giants, Philadelphia Eagles, St. Louis Cardinals, Washington Redskins, Chicago Bears, Detroit Lions, Green Bay Packers, Minnesota Vikings, Atlanta Falcons, Los Angeles Rams, New Orleans Saints, and San Francisco 49ers)
  • 1971 - San Diego Rockets move to Houston
  • 1972 - Atlanta Flames and New York Islanders (both NHL) created as expansion teams; World Hockey Association is established; Cincinnati Royals move to Kansas City and become the Kings
  • 1973 - Baltimore Bullets move to Washington
  • 1974 - Kansas City Scouts (NHL), New Orleans Jazz (NBA), and Washington Capitals (NHL) created as expansion teams
  • 1976 - ABA-NBA merger; six ABA teams absorbed to NBA: Denver Nuggets, Indiana Pacers, Kentucky Colonels, New Jersey Nets, San Antonio Spurs, and Spirits of St. Louis; Kansas City Scouts move to Denver and become the Colorado Rockies; California Golden Seals move to Cleveland and become the Barons; Seattle Seahawks and Tampa Bay Buccaneers (both NFC) created as expansion teams; Birmingham Vulcans and Memphis Grizzlies (both AFL) join NFL from WFL
  • 1977 - Kansas City Royals (MLB AL), Montreal Expos (MLB NL), San Diego Padres (MLB NL), Seattle Mariners (MLB AL), and Virginia Squires (NBA) created as expansion teams
  • 1978 - Cleveland Barons merge with Minnesota North Stars; Buffalo Braves move to San Diego and become the Clippers
  • 1979 - New Orleans Jazz move to Salt Lake City and become the Utah Jazz; NHL-WHA merger; six WHA teams absorbed into NHL: Edmonton Oilers, Cincinnati Stingers, Houston Aeros, Hartford Whalers, Quebec Nordiques, and Winnipeg Jets
  • 1980 - Dallas Mavericks (NBA) created as expansion team; Atlanta Flames move to Calgary
  • 1982 - Oakland Raiders move to Los Angeles; Colorado Rockies move to Newark and become the New Jersey Devils;
  • 1984 - Baltimore Colts move to Indianapolis
  • 1988 - St. Louis Cardinals (NFL) move to Phoenix and become the Arizona Cardinals; Virginia Squires move to Sacramento and become the Senators
  • 1989 - Charlotte Hornets, Miami Heat, Minnesota Timberwolves, and Orlando Magic (all NBA) created as expansion teams
  • 1991 - San Jose Sharks (NHL) created as expansion team
  • 1992 - Colorado Rockies (MLB), Miami Marlins (MLB) Ottawa Senators (NHL), and Tampa Bay Lightning (NHL) created as expansion teams
  • 1993 - Minnesota North Stars move to Dallas; Anaheim Ducks and Florida Panthers (both NHL) created as expansion teams
  • 1995 - Los Angeles Raiders move back to Oakland; Colorado Avalanche (NHL), Toronto Raptors (NBA), and Vancouver Grizzlies (NBA) created as expansion teams; Charlotte Panthers and Jacksonville Jaguars both join NFL from USFL
  • 1996 - Phoenix Coyotes (NHL) created as expansion team; Baltimor Ravens and St. Louis Steamers join NFL from USFL; Major League Soccer is established with ten teams: Columbus Crew, District United, Boston Revolution, New Jersey MetroStars, Tampa Bay Mutiny, Colorado Rapids, FC Dallas, Sporting Kansas City, Los Angeles Galaxy, and San Jose Quakes
  • 1997 - Memphis Grizzlies move to Salt Lake City and become the Utah Grizzlies; Nashville Predators and Hampton Roads Rhinos (both NHL) created as expansion teams
  • 1998 - Chicago Municipal (MLS), Miami Fusion (MLS), Carolina Hurricanes (NHL), and Portland Lumberjacks (NHL) created as expansion teams
  • 1999 - Arizona Diamondbacks (MLB), Atlanta Thrashers (NHL), and New Orleans Zephyrs (MLB) created as expansion teams; Oklahoma Outlaws (NFL) and Tennessee Titans (NFL) join NFL from USFL
  • 2000 - Minnesota Wildcats and Milwaukee Muskies (both NHL) created as expansion teams
  • 2001 - Tampa Bay Mutiny and Miami Fusion contracted; Nashville Sounds and Tampa Bay Rays (both MLB) created as expansion teams; Vancouver Grizzlies move to Memphis
  • 2002 - New Orleans Pelicans (NBA) created as expansion team; Honolulu Hawaiians, Hollywood Suns, Portland Breakers, and San Antonio Texans join NFL as part of NFL/USFL merger
  • 2004 - Virginia Tides (NBA) created as expansion team
  • 2005 - Chivas USA and Salt Lake Colony (both MLS) created as expansion teams
  • 2006 - Houston Dynamo (MLS) created as expansion team
  • 2007 - Kansas City Scouts (NHL) and Toronto FC (MLS) created as expansion team
  • 2008 - Oklahoma City Thunder and Pittsburgh Pipers (both NBA) created as expansion teams
  • 2009 - Seattle Sounders (MLS) created as expansion team
  • 2010 - Philadelphia Union (MLS) created as expansion team
  • 2011 - Portland Timbers and Vancouver Whitecaps (both MLS) created as expansion teams; Hampton Roads Rhinos move to Hamilton and become the Tigers
  • 2012 - Montreal Impact (MLS), Las Vegas Novas (MLB), and Vancouver Mounties (MLB) created as expansion teams
  • 2014 - Chivas USA contracted; Carolina Pilots and Portland Beavers (both MLB) created as expansion teams
  • 2015 - New York City FC and Orlando City (both MLS) created as expansion teams
  • 2017 - Terminus Atlanta (MLS), Las Vegas Knights (NHL), Twin Cities United (MLS), and Seattle Evergreens (NHL) created as expansion teams
  • 2018 - Los Angeles FC (MLS) created as expansion team
  • 2019 - FC Cincinnati (MLS) created as expansion team
  • 2020 - Las Vegas Vipers (NBA), Omaha Owls (NBA), Inter Miami (MLS), Montreal Royals (NBA), and Nashville SC (MLS) created as expansion teams
  • 2021 - Austin SC (MLS) created as expansion team
  • 2022 - One Carolina FC (MLS) created as expansion team
  • 2023 - AC St. Louis and Sacramento Republic (both MLS) created as expansion teams
  • 2024 - Las Vegas Lights and Phoenix Rising (both MLS) created as expansion teams
  • 2027 - Detroit Motor SC, Indy Eleven, San Diego Naval, and Tampa Bay Rowdies (all MLS) created as expansion teams
  • 2030 - New Mexico United, Louisville Premier, Oklahoma City FC, and Three Rivers United (all MLS) created as expansion teams
